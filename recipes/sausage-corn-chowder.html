Sausage Corn Chowder

Description

This dish is a particular favorite of mine. Though perhaps not a 'traditional' chowder, it is delicious, simple to make, and does not take much time or too many ingredients. Perhaps the most marvelous aspect of this recipe is that it serves as a template for chowders. The reader can at the appropriate stage substitute the sausage or corn for whichever protein and/or vegetable he or she desires.Also, a fish stock is preferrable if the protein is seafood.

Ingredients

  • 1 Tbs Butter
  • 1 Tbs cooking oil
  • 3 cups of diced onions
  • 1 bay leaf
  • 1 cup of flour
  • 4 cups of light chicken stock
  • 1 pound of chopped, peeled potatoes.
  • Salt and Pepper to taste
  • 2 cups of milk or light cream
  • 1 package of Italian sausages
  • 2 cups of frozen corn

Steps

  1. Prep. Gather all ingredients. Peel and chop potatoes and onions. Remove sausages from cases.
  2. Heat stew pot or dutch oven over medium-high heat. Add cooking oil, then add sausages. Break up the sausages into as small of pieces as possible while they cook. Once browned and partially cooked, remove sausages to separate plate.
  3. Melt the butter in the pot. Stir in the onions and bay leaf. Saute over medium-low heat until onions are tender and translucent which should take no more than 10 minutes.
  4. Add the four cups of liquid to the pot. Bring to a boil and let it simmer, loosely covered, until potatoes are soft and easily pierced by a fork.
  5. Create a slurry with the flour and water. Add the slurry to the pot and stir vigorously to prevent clumps. Continue stirring until slurry is well incorporated and the chowder thickens.
  6. Mix in the milk/cream, corn, and sausage. Bring to a simmer for five minutes. While simmering, use a potato masher to mash the potatoes in the pot into smaller and more manageable chunks. Taste to correct seasoning.
